So why did we believe that eating dietary fat somehow magically bypassed digestion and went straight to our bellies? In the 1980s, the "Low-Fat" craze took over the world. We ripped the fat out of yogurt, milk, and snacks.

But when you remove fat, food tastes like cardboard. So food companies replaced the fat with massive amounts of **sugar**. The result? The greatest rise in obesity and diabetes in human history. To fix our diets, we have to start by completely resetting how we view fat and cholesterol.

## Let's talk about *Cholesterol*

Think of your body like a house being built. **Cholesterol is the literal bricks and mortar.**

It isn't a poison; it's a vital structural component. Every single cell in your body needs cholesterol to build its outer wall. What's more important: your sex hormones (like testosterone and estrogen) are literally manufactured out of cholesterol. Without fat, your hormones crash. Your brain, by the way, is made up of about 60% fat.

<div class="callout">
  <p>"When you eat a low-fat diet, you essentially tell your brain and your hormones to stop paying their biological bills because there's no money in the bank."</p>
</div>

## The Delivery Truck Analogy (HDL vs LDL)

But wait—if cholesterol is so good, why does my doctor test my LDL and HDL?

Cholesterol is like oil, and your blood is like water. Oil and water don't mix. If you drop cholesterol directly into the blood, it clumps up. To solve this, your liver packages the cholesterol into little "submarines" or "trucks" called lipoproteins so they can float through the bloodstream.

- **LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein):** This is the **Delivery Truck**. It carries fresh cholesterol from your liver out to your cells so they can build walls and hormones.
- **HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein):** This is the **Garbage Truck**. It goes around picking up unused or old cholesterol and brings it back to the liver to be recycled.

## So when is LDL actually "bad"?

LDL is just a delivery truck doing its job. It only becomes dangerous when the truck gets "damaged." What damages the truck?
  
**Sugar and Industrial Seed Oils.**

When you consume massive amounts of sugar and highly processed seed oils, it creates inflammation in the bloodstream. This inflammation oxidizes (damages) the LDL trucks. Imagine a damaged delivery truck crashing on the side of the highway. When enough damaged trucks crash against the walls of your blood vessels, it creates a traffic jam. *That* is plaque buildup. *That* is a heart attack.

**The fat didn't cause the heart attack. The sugar and the inflammation wrecked the truck.**
